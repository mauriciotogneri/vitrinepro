02 — The atelier

A rare craft, practised since 1985.

Jean-Robert Gase is a hatter and milliner in Geneva. He shapes felt, straw and coiled felt by hand — for women and men.

Trained as a ladies' tailor in a Geneva couture house, he specialised through several advanced courses before setting up as an independent hatter in 1985.

Since then, his Rue des Jardins atelier, in La Jonction, is where the pieces are born — one by one, by hand, on wooden blocks.

Distinction

“Artisan·e Métiers d'Art Genève — Atelier”

Jean-Robert Gase holds the Geneva Métiers d'Art (rare crafts) seal, which distinguishes workshops of rare crafts — open by appointment or on special occasions.

06 — Questions

Frequently asked questions.

How do I buy or order a hat?

By appointment at the Rue des Jardins atelier, or directly at the market stall — notably at the Espace de la Fusterie. The simplest is to contact me beforehand, by phone, email or WhatsApp.

Does the atelier have fixed opening hours?

No. It's a rare-craft workshop, open by appointment and on special occasions. Write or call, and we'll arrange a time that suits.

What do you make?

Handmade hats in felt, straw and coiled felt, plus caps — for women and men, in varied materials and colours.

Do you make pieces for both women and men?

Yes. Every piece is made for women as well as men.

Is there a price list?

As each piece is handmade, its price depends on the material and the work. It's best discussed at the atelier or the market — feel free to contact me for a quote.

Where can I find you at the markets?

I'm a regular at Geneva's markets, especially the Espace de la Fusterie. Days can vary: contact me to be sure of finding me.
