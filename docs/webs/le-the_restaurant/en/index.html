Over a hundred Chinese teas

Monsieur Lam, a tea specialist and author of Légendes du thé ("Legends of Tea"), has gathered over a hundred teas with poetic names, sorted into the major families. Every cup has its legend — ask him for it.

The house

A corner of China
on Rue des Bains

Le Thé fits inside one tiny room: nine tables, a bamboo canopy, carved-wood screens and purple-clay teapots on every wall.

Monsieur Lam, of Chinese-Vietnamese origin, is an architect by training turned tea specialist. Here he has gathered over a hundred Chinese teas and written Légendes du thé, a book retelling the legend behind each one. His wife, of Chinese-Malaysian origin, makes the pandan crêpes herself.

Since 1994, people have come for handmade dim sum and the yam-cha ritual. The address is a quiet secret and often full — best to book ahead.
