# Site Structure

Single-page site — one HTML file, every section on one scrollable page, linked by in-page anchors. Sections run top to bottom in the order below — include every one and keep that order; omit or reorder a section only when explicitly instructed otherwise. Don't number the sections — label each heading and eyebrow by name, never with an ordinal (no "02 — L'atelier" eyebrow, no "02" section-number badge). Give consecutive sections **distinct background tones** — an alternating banded rhythm drawn from the theme palette so each section is visually separated from its neighbours, never one flat background top to bottom (see `site-conventions.md` → **Section backgrounds**).

## Header / Nav bar

Sticky top bar, present on every screen — the site's primary navigation.

- **Logo / brand** — a mark (logo image or inline SVG) plus the business wordmark; links back to the top (`#top`).
- **Anchor nav** — one in-page link per major section, in document order. Labels are themed in fr-CH, not generic — "La carte", "La maison", "Nous trouver", never "Menu"/"About". The in-view section's link is marked on scroll (scrollspy — see `best-practices.md` → `aria-current` / `IntersectionObserver`).
- **Mobile toggle** — a hamburger button revealing the nav on small screens, wired per `best-practices.md` (`aria-expanded`, `aria-controls`, `aria-label`).

_No CTA button in the header — keep the bar minimal. The actual CTAs live in the hero, between sections._

## Hero

- **Eyebrow / overline** — tiny context label above the headline; a `Catégorie · quartier · Genève` kicker — "Café · Bar — Plainpalais, Genève", "Épicerie bio & santé · Jonction, Genève" — sometimes carrying a since-year ("depuis 1985", "Est. 2019"). Prefix it with an icon only when one clearly fits the business (e.g. a sprout for a bio épicerie) and is simple enough to read at that tiny size — a clean single-shape glyph, never a detailed mark or one with text/lettering in it; if none qualifies, show the text alone rather than force a generic, busy, or off-theme one.
- **Headline** — the one big promise, and the page's main SEO heading (`<h1>`); large display type. Written in fr-CH; it names the offering and, where it reads naturally, ties it to Geneva or the quartier ("Le Portugal fait maison, à la Jonction.", "Pizza au feu de bois & cuisine italienne à Genève") — but don't force a place in when the line is stronger without it ("Les matins ont meilleur goût ici."). Often broken across lines with `<br>` or accenting one highlighted word.
- **Subhead / lede** — usually a single sentence (two at most) expanding the promise; packs the specifics — signature products, since-year, neighborhood — in the brand's own voice.
- **CTAs** — one primary + one secondary button, themed in fr-CH and text-only — no icons in the buttons. Primary is the contact action ("Nous contacter", "Appeler le salon", "Prendre contact"); secondary points at the offering ("Voir la carte", "Voir les produits", "Découvrir la collection"). _Contact wording only — never "Réserver"/"Prendre rendez-vous" (see `site-conventions.md` → **CTA labels**)._
- **Trust / meta strip** — a tight, divider-separated row, filled in priority order: rating, then TripAdvisor rating, then the live open/closed status chip. Each rating (stars + score) shows only when it's 4/5 or higher — the first from whatever source the dossier has (typically Google), the second from TripAdvisor; a missing or sub-4/5 score is dropped, not shown. When a rating slot can't be filled, the address takes its place (shown once, even if both ratings are missing). The live open/closed status chip (JS-rendered from the opening hours; see `site-conventions.md` → **Open/closed status chip**) is always present, last.
- **Visual** — the LCP hero photo (loaded per `site-conventions.md` → **LCP photo**), usually paired with decorative SVG — a seal, brush stroke, scattered doodles, or a script-character watermark; occasionally a small multi-photo collage instead of a single image. If the dossier has an official logo or logomark that sits cleanly over the photo, overlay it small in the bottom-left corner of the image; omit it when it doesn't read well there.

## Services / Menu / Products

The first content section after the hero and the page's centre of gravity: a clear, scannable account of what the business offers. There's no single template — name and shape it to the trade: a **carte** (food), a grid of **produits / rayons / collections** (shops), a list of **prestations / soins** (services), a **collection** (second-hand). The nav links to it with a themed fr-CH label — "La carte", "La table", "Produits", "Les soins", "Le métier", "La collection".

- **Section head** — an eyebrow that echoes the nav label or sets the scene ("La carte", "Au comptoir", "Nos rayons", "Nos prestations"); an evocative fr-CH `<h2>`, never the bare word "Menu" ("Tout droit sorti du four", "À midi, on passe à table.", "Les spécialités de la maison", "Dim sum, douceurs & thés"); and a one- to two-sentence lede in the brand's voice — what's made, "fait maison", service hours, dishes "à partager".
- **Layout — fit the trade, don't force one template.** Three base shapes recur: items grouped under a few **named categories** ("Pour commencer", "Les classiques", "Spécialités", "Douceurs & bar"); a **flat list of dish / product _families_** (one elegant card, or a classic dotted-leader list — the leader running to a tag, never to a price); or a **grid of icon cards** — each a department/family with a line-art SVG glyph, a title, and one sentence — the default for shops and service businesses with no fixed menu ("rayons", "collections", "soins"). Richer variants seen across the sites: a day-to-night split, chalkboard "boards", or a single **signature item** given a spotlight.
- **Food / drink menus** — the go-to shape is a **named-category carte**: a two-column grid of a few category blocks ("Le salé", "La boulangerie", "La pâtisserie", "Au comptoir"), each a heading (optionally with a small glyph) over a hairline-divided list of dishes. Every dish is a **name + one warm, descriptive line**, plus the occasional inline tag ("spécialité", "maison", "chaque jour") — editorial, never a spec sheet. **When the carte runs long, split the categories into tabs** instead of one endless scroll: a row of category buttons above the list, only the selected category's **panel shown at a time**, swapped on click with a quiet fade and the first category open on load. Wire the buttons accessibly (a `tablist` of `tab`s controlling `tabpanel`s with `aria-selected`, or buttons with `aria-pressed`) and degrade to the full list when scripting is off.
- **Each item** — a **name + a short description** in the brand's voice, always. Add a small **tag** only to flag a signature or notable item ("Signature", "Maison", "Le classique", "Végé") — sparingly, one or two per list, never a full dietary-marker system.
- **No prices here — keep the section price-free.** No per-item prices, no price list, no ranges or "à partir de" / "dès …" figures — not even for a barber or a fixed café carte. Pricing is addressed only by the closing note's polite deferral to the shop or a direct contact (below), never inline against an item. _(No durations either — drop any "30 min"-style label; even the barber and groomer show none.)_
- **No images here — text and icon only.** The section shows **no photos** — no per-item photos, no captioned photo tiles, no hero-style imagery. The only graphics are the decorative line-art **SVG glyphs / icons** (card marks, bullets, eyebrow glyphs) — never a photograph. The real dish / product / work photos belong to **About** (its feature photo and gallery), not this section.
- **Closing note** — a small footnote usually ends the list, carrying the **pricing posture** (always a deferral, never a figure: "Les prix sont affichés en boutique", "communiqués au comptoir", "Pas de tarifs en ligne", a "devis personnalisé" for quote-based services), the **living-menu caveat** ("carte indicative", "elle évolue au fil des saisons", "au fil des arrivages"), and any **payment / service facts** ("Cartes acceptées", "Espèces uniquement", "À emporter, livraison, sur place", "Sans rendez-vous").

_No prominent CTA button inside this section — the "see the offering" CTA lives in the hero and scrolls in (see **Hero**). The section closes on its note. Any link here stays contact- or offering-worded, never "Réserver" (see `site-conventions.md` → **CTA labels**)._

## About

The brand's story — the major content block after Services and the page's credibility centre. The nav links to it with a themed fr-CH label drawn from the trade and the place — "La maison" (the default, fits almost any business), "La boutique", "L'atelier", "Le salon", "L'ambiance", "Notre histoire" — never "À propos"/"About". It's built as a two-column **about-grid** — the story column on the **left**, a single large feature photo on the **right** (always that orientation, never reversed) — with the photo gallery, when present, as a full-width block below.

- **Section head** — an eyebrow echoing the nav label, occasionally prefixed with a small line-art glyph (a swirl, a dot, a star); and an evocative fr-CH `<h2>`, never the bare "À propos" ("Une maison de famille", "Une table italienne à la Jonction", "Un coin de Chine, rue des Bains", "Un salon à taille humaine, tenu avec cœur"). An opening **lede**, set larger than the body, often leads the story.
- **Story** — two or three short paragraphs in the brand's voice: where it sits (quartier + street — "Au cœur de la Jonction", "boulevard Carl-Vogt"), who's behind it (the named owner or family, with a since-year where there is one), what it makes and how ("fait maison", "au feu de bois", "à la main"), and the feel of the place — the room, the materials, the welcome. A signature detail often closes it. Keep it specific to the dossier — no generic "passion & quality" filler, and keep the rating out of the prose (scores live in the hero trust strip and Reviews, not the story).
- **Team** — usually the **people woven into the story prose** as a named owner or family ("menée par Christophe Dubois", "tenue par Aamir Ali", "tenu par Alexandra"), not a separate roster. A dedicated **team module** — small cards with an initials avatar, a name and a role — is the exception, for co-owners worth crediting side by side ("Catherine Ryser · Associée-gérante"). A signed **sign-off** is a common lighter touch in the same spirit ("Avec soin, Alexandra · Toiletteuse", "— à demain matin ☕"). Name only who the dossier supports; prefer prose to a thin one-person "team", and never invent people.
- **Feature chips** — a compact strip of practical amenity chips, each a short fr-CH label carrying **its own small line-art icon**, ideally one that depicts the amenity (a wheelchair, a Wi-Fi arc, a terrasse, a paw, a payment card); fall back to a plain check only when no fitting glyph exists. Chips are the **only** highlights shape here — no stat / big-number blocks, no definition lists. Draw them from the dossier's practical facts: accessibility ("Accès fauteuil roulant"), Wi-Fi, parking, payment methods ("Cartes acceptées", "Paiement sans contact"), climatisation, terrasse, livraison, à emporter, animaux admis, "LGBTQ+ friendly" — and the like (not limited to these). **Never chip a rating, a location / quartier, or a price** — ratings belong to the hero trust strip and Reviews, the address to the Location section, and pricing stays a deferral in the Services closing note.
- **Feature photo** — the about-grid's right column is **one single large photo** — never a collage or a mini-grid here — the visual counterweight to the story. A dual-format `<picture>` per `site-conventions.md` → **Images** (WebP + JPEG, explicit `width`/`height`, `loading="lazy"`, `decoding="async"`, a descriptive fr-CH `alt`), click-to-enlarge per `site-conventions.md` → **Image enlargement (lightbox)**. Usually **uncaptioned** — the story carries the words; a light theme accent is welcome. It stacks under the copy on narrow screens.
- **Photo gallery** — an **optional, separate full-width block after the about-grid** (never folded into the grid's photo column), and the home for the rest of the photography (Services is text-and-icon only; its dish / product / work photos live here). A **mosaic of `<figure>`s** — often one larger tile among smaller ones — each a dual-format `<picture>` with the same image + lightbox conventions as the feature photo, plus a short, evocative **`<figcaption>`** ("La devanture, boulevard Carl-Vogt", "Théières de Yixing", "Repulgue tressé à la main") — captions sometimes `sr-only` on dense tiles. Subjects: the space, the storefront, the products / dishes, the work — and, where the trade calls for it, a different cast (a groomer's freshly-clipped "Portraits", a hatter's market étal). Typically 3–9 photos; omit the gallery when the dossier has only enough strong photos for the feature image.
  - **Placement** — either stacked **below** the story+photo grid under a small "En images" sub-head, or **promoted to its own standalone section** right after, with its own eyebrow + `<h2>` and nav link (e.g. a "Galerie" or "Portraits" nav label). Promote it when the set is strong enough to stand alone (a restaurant's plates, an artisan's pieces); keep it a stacked sub-block when it's a supporting accent.

## Reviews

Social proof, and the page's credibility echo right after About: the dossier's rating shown large, with a handful of real customer quotes. The nav links to it with a themed fr-CH label — all but universally **"Avis"** (never "Témoignages"/"Reviews"); it sits between About and Location/Contact.

**This is the one section whose presence is data-gated, not fixed** — include it only when the dossier earns it: a genuine aggregate rating **4/5 or higher** (the same bar the hero trust strip uses — see **Hero**) _and_ at least two or three quotable review texts. When the dossier has neither — rating consent-gated or absent, no readable reviews — **omit the whole section**.

- **Section head** — an eyebrow ("Vos avis", "Les avis", "Ils en parlent", "Ce qu'on en dit", "vos petits mots"), occasionally carrying a small star glyph or dot; and a warm, evocative fr-CH `<h2>`, never the bare "Avis" ("Ce que vous en dites", "Les habitués sont formels", "On y revient pour l'ambiance.", "La table, vue par nos hôtes", "Un secret bien gardé").
- **Source ratings** — summarise the dossier's standing **per platform**. Each qualifying source gets its own summary: the **global score out of 5** (fr-CH decimal comma — "4,7 / 5") with a **star row** (full stars plus a **fractional last star** — a clipped half via `<span class="half">` or a partial-fill SVG; a plain empty ☆ when the score rounds down) and the **review count + platform** ("504 avis · Google", "39 avis · TripAdvisor"). **Only show a source whose own score is 4/5 or higher** — drop any platform below that bar entirely and never average it into another's (a 3,8 TripAdvisor row simply doesn't appear). Lead with the strongest source, Google before TripAdvisor, mirroring the hero strip's priority (see **Hero**). **Make each summary a link** that opens that platform's public listing in a new tab (`target="_blank" rel="noopener"`) whenever the dossier has the URL — plain text otherwise. Show as many qualifying sources as the dossier carries — they're **not mutually exclusive**; eligible platforms include Google, TripAdvisor, Facebook, local.ch, search.ch, Restaurant Guru, and the like (not limited to these). A non-scored **distinction may ride along**, exempt from the 4/5 filter — a TripAdvisor **"Travelers' Choice"** badge, or a **category ranking** ("16ᵉ sur 84 restaurants chinois à Genève", "209ᵉ / 1 882 à Genève"). Several qualifying sources → one summary row/card each (a stat-row); just one or two → a single folded line is fine ("63 avis Google · 4,5 sur Just Eat").
- **Review cards** — below the summary, the **curated best reviews** (typically three to six, drawn from the qualifying sources above) — always the **most meaningful, representative** ones for the business, not merely the newest — each in its **own card**. **When the cards wrap to more than one row, keep every row equally filled** — choose a count that divides evenly across the grid (e.g. 3 or 6 in a three-up grid), never an orphan card alone on a short last row. Each card carries, in order:
  - an **avatar** — a plain **monogram of the reviewer's first-name initial** (a single "A"); **never the reviewer's real photo or platform avatar**, the initial only.
  - a **decorative quote glyph** (a large “…” quotation mark or a quote-mark SVG).
  - the review's **star rating**.
  - the **comment** — the verbatim excerpt (one to three sentences), kept in **its original language** when that's how it was posted (an English Google review stays English).
  - the reviewer's **name** — real, from the dossier, **never invented** (a first name + initial, or the handle exactly as posted; anonymise to "Cliente" only when the source gives no name).
  - the **source** — the platform the review came from ("Google", "TripAdvisor", "Restaurant Guru").

_No contact form, booking widget, or CTA button here — and **never a "leave / post a review" button or link to any platform** ("Laisser un avis", "Écrire un avis", "Donnez votre avis"): the section is read-only social proof, and the only outbound links are the per-source rating summaries above (which open the listing to **read**, never to post). Give it a distinct background band — Reviews is one of the sections that can carry a deep "feature" band (`site-conventions.md` → **Section backgrounds**). The visible scores, counts and reviews are mirrored into the page's `LocalBusiness` JSON-LD as `aggregateRating` + `review`, visible-content-backed (`site-conventions.md` → **JSON-LD**)._

## Location

The practical "come find us" block — address, map, opening hours and the know-before-you-go facts — and, with **Contact**, the closing pair that ends the page. It's near-universal; only the most minimal businesses fold it into Contact (see the closing note). The nav links to it with a themed fr-CH label — **"Nous trouver"** the clear default, with **"Visite"** or **"Accès"** the common alternates — never "Localisation"/"Plan". It's a **two-column block: an info _card_ on the left, the embedded Google map on the right — the map must always be on the right.** On narrow screens the map stacks under the card.

- **Section head** — an eyebrow echoing the nav label or warming it ("Nous trouver", "Accès", "Nous rendre visite", "Où & quand", "venez nous dire bonjour"); and an fr-CH `<h2>` that most often **names the address or the quartier** rather than a bare "Adresse" — the street ("Au 49, boulevard Carl-Vogt", "Avenue de Sainte-Clotilde 18") or the neighbourhood ("Au cœur de la Jonction", "Au cœur du Quartier des Bains") — though it may simply restate the label ("Nous trouver", "Accès & horaires"). An optional one-line lede sometimes sets the scene ("À emporter, au cœur de Genève. Voici quand et où nous retrouver.").
- **The info card (left)** — a single card holding **one piece of information per row**, in this order:
  - **Address** — the street address (street, postcode, city), usually in an `<address>` and frequently with the **quartier appended** ("1205 Genève · Quartier de la Jonction", "1202 Genève — Servette"), rendered as a **clickable Google Maps link**, never plain text.
  - **"Voir l'itinéraire" button** — a dedicated directions button of its own, on top of the clickable address. _Its exact form — a `dir/?api=1` directions link (never a `?q=` "open in Maps" link), reusing the `<lat>,<lng>` of the embed — is in `site-conventions.md` → **Contact & location**._
  - **Opening hours** — **one line per day, every day on its own row — never grouped or compressed** (no "Lun – Ven · …"), even when consecutive days share the same hours. Closed days read "Fermé"; restaurants put split lunch/dinner service on the day's line ("12:00–14:30 · 18:30–22:30"). **Today's row is marked and carries the live open/closed status** — the same `Ouvert · ferme à 22:00` / `Fermé · ouvre à 09:00` text the hero trust strip shows, rendered by `js/main.js` in `Europe/Zurich` (see `site-conventions.md` → **Open/closed status chip**). A small living-hours caveat may follow ("Les horaires peuvent varier les jours fériés — appelez-nous avant de venir").
    - **By-appointment trades** show "Sur rendez-vous" in place of clock times on the day rows ("Sur rendez-vous (dès 8h)"), pointing to the contact path to book.
  - **Access** _(only when the dossier has it)_ — strictly **how to reach and get into the place**, nothing else: **wheelchair / step-free access** ("Accès fauteuil roulant", "Accès de plain-pied (PMR)"), **public transport** — naming the actual lines and stops, not a vague "bien desservi" ("Tram 12 ou 15, arrêt Plainpalais ou Bains", "À 2 min de l'arrêt Dancet"), **parking** ("Parking à proximité"), and **any other getting-there or getting-in detail** the dossier gives — a building-entrance or floor note ("Atelier en sous-sol"), a drop-off point, and the like; the list isn't exhaustive. **State the honest negatives** rather than hide them — "Salle non accessible en fauteuil roulant". _Keep amenities **out** of this row — Wi-Fi, terrasse, animaux admis, payment methods and service modes (à emporter / livraison) are not shown here; those belong to **About**'s feature chips._
- **The map (right)** — the **Google Maps embed**, visible on load (no click-to-load button), in a fixed box with a descriptive fr-CH `title` ("Carte — Le Thé, Rue des Bains 65, 1205 Genève"). _The embed mechanics and the recurring map-overflow bug live in `site-conventions.md` → **Contact & location**._

_Give it a **distinct background band** from its neighbours like every section — Location is one that can carry a deep/inverted "feature" band (see `site-conventions.md` → **Section backgrounds**). The visible address, geo and hours are mirrored into the page's `LocalBusiness` JSON-LD (`address` / `geo` / `openingHoursSpecification`), visible-content-backed (`site-conventions.md` → **JSON-LD**). **Location and Contact are the closing pair — keep them adjacent;** on the most minimal businesses (a walk-in barber, a small café) the two can collapse into a single Contact section, dropping the standalone map._

## Contact

The page's primary conversion point, and — with **Location** — the closing pair that ends the page. The nav links to it with a themed fr-CH label, almost always **"Contact"**. It's a **two-column tile: the direct contact channels on the left, the contact form on the right** (always that orientation, DOM order matching the visual order; the canonical Formspree form lives in `site-conventions.md` → **Contact & location**), stacking on narrow screens. Like every section it takes a **distinct background tone**, and is one that can carry a deep/inverted "feature" band (see `site-conventions.md` → **Section backgrounds**).

- **Left column** — leads with the **section head** (an eyebrow — "Contact" the default, sometimes warmed to "Nous écrire" — and a warm fr-CH `<h2>`, rarely the bare word: "Une question ? Écrivez-nous", "Passez nous voir, ou écrivez-nous", "Prendre contact.") and a **small description**: a one- to two-sentence lede in the brand's voice ("Une question, une commande spéciale, un mot gentil ? Appelez-nous, ou laissez un message ci-dessous."). Below it, the **direct channels as clickable cards** — an icon-led list where each card is a single link carrying a line-art glyph, a small label and the value. Include **only the channels the dossier has**, in this order:
  - **Phone** — _only when the number is a landline_ (a Swiss geographic line, `0[2-6]x`): a clickable `tel:` card. A landline never gets a WhatsApp card — you can't WhatsApp a fixed line.
  - **WhatsApp** — _only when the number is a mobile_ (Swiss `07x` — 076/077/078/079): a clickable `https://wa.me/<intl>` card (optionally a prefilled `?text=`) shown **instead of** a `tel:` link — a mobile is offered over WhatsApp, not as a call. A business with both a landline and a mobile shows both: the landline as a phone card, the mobile as a WhatsApp card. _(A mobile the dossier flags as not on WhatsApp falls back to a `tel:` card.)_
  - **E-mail** — a clickable `mailto:` card.
  - **Address** — a clickable card opening Google Maps directions (`dir/?api=1&destination=<lat>,<lng>`, reusing the map's coords; see `site-conventions.md` → **Contact & location**), never plain text.
  - **Social links** — Instagram / Facebook and the like, **one per row**, each its own clickable card (only profiles the dossier shows are public; see `site-conventions.md` → **Social links**).
- **Right column — the contact form.** The **Formspree** name / e-mail / message form per `site-conventions.md` → **Contact & location** — a plain contact form, **never a reservation/booking widget, date / time-slot, party-size or appointment field**, even for restaurants and by-appointment trades. Its fields: **every field is mandatory** (all `required`, each label carrying a visible `*` marker) and **every field shows a hint** (a `placeholder` — "Votre nom", "vous@exemple.ch", "Votre message, une question, un mot…"). Keep the form bare: **no subtitle** above the fields (no form heading or intro line) and **nothing static below the submit button** ("Envoyer le message") — no response-time, privacy or reservation note beneath it. The one exception is the **dynamic** success/error status region injected by `references/snippets/contact-form.js` (see `site-conventions.md` → **Contact & location**): collapsed to zero height and invisible until a submit attempt resolves, so it adds no static content — it isn't the kind of note this rule forbids.
- **Trade framing — no booking, ever.** A **by-appointment** trade (tailor, groomer), a **walk-in** one (barber), or a **reservation-taking restaurant** keeps its steer — "sur rendez-vous", "sans rendez-vous", "la réservation se fait par téléphone" — in the **left description**, pointing to the phone or WhatsApp, since nothing sits below the form. Never a booking widget, and per `site-conventions.md` → **CTA labels** no CTA is ever worded "Réserver".

_The visible phone, e-mail and address are mirrored into the page's `LocalBusiness` JSON-LD (`telephone` / `email` / `address`), visible-content-backed (`site-conventions.md` → **JSON-LD**)._

## Footer

A **fixed three-column top region**, a **horizontal divider**, then a **two-part bottom bar** — the same on every site. Like every section it takes its own distinct background band (`site-conventions.md` → **Section backgrounds**); the three columns stack to one on narrow screens.

**Top region — three columns, left to right:**

- **Left — brand** — the **same logo/mark as the header nav**, linking back to the top (`#top`); a **short one-line description** of the business; and the **social links as icons** — inline SVG glyphs (Facebook, Instagram, and the like) via an inline SVG `<symbol>` sprite referenced by `<use>` (e.g. `#ico-fb` / `#ico-insta`), never text labels. _(Only link a profile the dossier shows is public — omit a private Instagram.)_
- **Center — "Contact"** — a **"Contact"** heading, then each on its own line: the **business name**, the **address** as a **clickable Google Maps link** (link form per `site-conventions.md` → **Contact & location**), the **phone** (`tel:`), and the **e-mail** (`mailto:`). Include only the channels the dossier has.
- **Right — "Horaires"** — a **"Horaires"** heading, then the **opening hours one line per day — never grouped or compressed**: every day on its own row, "Fermé" for closed days, split lunch/dinner service on the day's line — exactly as the **Location** section renders them.

**Divider** — a horizontal rule between the top region and the bottom bar.

**Bottom bar — two parts:**

- **Left** — the copyright line: `© <année> <Business> · <adresse> ·` then a **"Mentions légales"** link to `legal.html` (`target="_blank" rel="noopener"`; see `site-conventions.md` → **Legal page**) — e.g. "© 2026 Toutou-Club · Route de Meyrin 6, 1202 Genève · Mentions légales". The year is **JS-rendered** (`<span data-year>` / `<span id="year">`), never hardcoded; everything but "Mentions légales" is plain text.
- **Right** — the **"Site réalisé par VitrinePro"** credit, with **only the word "VitrinePro" hyperlinked** (not the whole phrase) to **`https://vitrinepro.ch`** (`target="_blank" rel="noopener"`; link form + target fixed by `site-conventions.md` → **Footer credit**).

_(No secondary-nav column and no language switcher, and no inline privacy/cookie text (it lives on the legal page). The legal **form** may ride along in the copyright name ("… Sàrl", "Entreprise individuelle"), but **never** a company-registration or tax ID — no UID / `CHE-…` / TVA — anywhere. The footer's name, address, phone, e-mail and hours stay consistent with the **Location** / **Contact** sections and the page's `LocalBusiness` JSON-LD.)_
